1. What you’re actually building

Not “an AI therapist.”
You’re building:

An AQAL-informed, topographic self-mirroring system
that helps humans see their own terrain clearly and then work with it, ideally alongside human therapists.

So the LLM is:

a cartographer (maps quadrants, levels, lines, states, types),

a mirror (reflects patterns back non-defensively), and

a protocol engine (suggests practices and micro-interventions).

Because AI “therapy” is still unregulated and controversial, this is also legally/ethically cleaner: current AI mental-health apps are framed as support, coaching and journaling, not as clinical therapy or replacements for therapists.

2. Using AQAL as the core schema (your “topography”)

AQAL gives you a beautifully opinionated data model:
all quadrants, all levels, all lines, all states, all types.

You can literally turn that into your internal ontology.

2.1 Quadrants → data streams

UL (Interior-Individual):

Journals, free-text sessions with the LLM

Beliefs, fears, narratives, dreams, values

UR (Exterior-Individual):

Sleep, steps, HRV, meds, nutrition, symptom trackers (from wearables or manual logs)

LL (Interior-Collective):

Family, culture, religion, organisational stories

“My parents always said…”, “In our community we…”

LR (Exterior-Collective):

Job role, income, housing, work structures, healthcare access

Systems constraints: time, money, location, responsibilities

Implementation idea:
Every user utterance gets multi-label classification across these quadrants. The LLM tags segments of text as UL/LL, etc, and stores them in your user graph.

2.2 Levels → altitude bands

You don’t want to hard-label people “Blue/Orange/Green,” but you can:

Use soft inferences (probabilities) of developmental altitude in different lines (e.g. self-identity, relationships, spirituality).

Represent this visually as altitude bands rather than “you are Stage X.”

This mirrors integral psychotherapy’s use of developmental levels to contextualise symptoms and growth.

2.4 States & types

States: track transient moods and state shifts (“right now I feel…”; peak states; trauma activations).

Types: optional overlays (e.g. attachment style, Big Five, Enneagram) used as lenses, not identities.

All of this becomes the schema for your internal user model.

3. The “topographic LLM” stack – in roles

Instead of one monolithic bot, think in specialised agents:

The Cartographer (Analysis Agent)

Takes user text + contextual data, updates their AQAL map.

Tasks:

Quadrant tagging

Level/line inference (soft)

Type and attachment inferences

Pattern detection (“this theme has appeared 5 times in 2 weeks”).

The Mirror (Reflective Agent)

Speaks to the user.

Outputs:

Non-judgmental reflections (“Here’s how your week looks across your inner and outer life…”)

Gentle pattern statements (“I notice work shows up in LR + UL as pressure and self-worth themes.”)

Clarifying questions.

The Guide (Protocol Agent)

Suggests evidence-based micro-interventions, framed through AQAL.

Example:

UL: cognitive reframing, emotional labelling, self-compassion practices

UR: sleep hygiene, breathing, basic behaviour activation

LL: communication scripts, boundary-setting experiments

LR: calendar experiments, system redesign (workflows, habits)

Draws from CBT, ACT, IFS, mindfulness etc, but matched to the user’s altitude and line strengths.

The Supervisor (Safety Agent)

Runs in the background; detects risk (self-harm, psychosis flags, abuse) and:

switches into crisis-protocol messaging

4. Data & model architecture (kernel/syntax layer)

Treat the human as:

A living stateful graph with AQAL as the schema, and each interaction as syntax updating that graph.

Edges: “conflict with”, “idealises”, “avoids”, “depends on”.

Properties: quadrant tags, line scores, timestamps, states.

5. MVP: what to build first

To avoid boiling the ocean, a coherent MVP could be:

Guided journaling + AQAL tagging

User writes daily or ad-hoc entries.

Cartographer tags quadrants, themes, and approximate lines.

Mirror reflects:

“Here’s how today’s entry distributes across your inner world, your body/behaviour, your relationships, and your systems.”

Weekly “Integral Reflection”

Automatically generate a weekly report:

Key themes per quadrant

Recurring patterns (e.g. “self-criticism around productivity shows up in UL and LR”)

2–3 practices across UL/UR/LL/LR.

Micro-interventions library

A small, vetted library of practices, each annotated with:

Quadrant(s)

Level(s) it’s appropriate for

Contraindications (e.g., trauma-sensitive flags).

Hard safety rails

Crisis phrases immediately trigger:

“I’m an AI and not a substitute for emergency help.”

Country-appropriate emergency and help-line signposting.

This is enough to demonstrate the topographic / AQAL value without pretending to be a full therapy replacement.
